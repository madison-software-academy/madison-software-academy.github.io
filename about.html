About the MSA

Your Support

6 Hours of Personal Mentorship per month

You'll have a mentor you can contact to get you through the difficult parts. Each mentor is an experienced Ruby on Rails developer with a vested interest in helping you succeed.

Advice from our Training Coordinator

Along with the personal mentor for technical questions, you will be supported by an advisor who will help through the course and also prepare you for the graduation and hiring process at the end.

Membership to both MadWorks and Horizon Co-working

Many developers work remotely and utilize the local co-working offices. We will pay for your membership in MadWorks and Horizon Co-working. And we'll host coding meetups at these coworking places to help you network with other developers.

Bi-weekly Student Get-Togethers

We host coding meetups at the coworking offices on a bi-weekly basis. Come meet some of the mentors and other students, as well as other local developers in Madison. These are great for learning what it is like to be a professional developer and you'll be introduced to some of the more experienced developers in Madison.

4 hours of Professional Design

You'll be required to finish a capstone project in order to graduate. These projects are what are shown the employers. They can be an idea you have or a project that has been requested by someone in the Madison civic community. We provide professional designers to help make your capstone project look fantastic and professional - which is a huge help when you show it to a potential employer.

Employer Connections

We have a very vested interest in connecting you with employers upon graduation. We work hard in helping you construct a great online resume and presence in order to become a professional developer. It is our primary goal.

Student Forums

You'll be able to give and receive help from your peers who are taking the course with you. Discuss projects, sticking points, and study & work techniques.

Curriculum

Implementing From Day One

Many other bootcamps talk about learning by doing, but at MSA it is reality. From day one you will be deploying a web application hosted on Heroku. You'll continually add to that application, learning Ruby, RSpec, Behavior Driven Development, git, Rails and other important skills firms are looking to hire. We don't even start teaching theory until after you've deployed several web applications.

Mid curriculum is when we start a dive into theory. This is a better approach than other schools (and standard academia) because the theory will make more sense when you have an understanding of its application - this is the key to successful learning and one of the advantages we offer our students.
